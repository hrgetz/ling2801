Controversy Investigation

Scientists don’t fully understand how language works in the brain, and there are many areas of controversy and debate. Throughout the semester, in groups of 2 or 3, you will:

  • choose an area of controversy to investigate;
  • read a review/opinion piece explaining the issue;
  • study the evidence yourself, as reported in the primary literature;
  • come up with an idea for a follow-up study that would contribute new relevant evidence; and
  • present your findings and ideas to the class.

Final deliverable

I’m open to a variety of formats for your final deliverable. Some examples: a short-form video for TikTok or Youtube, a longer educational video, a podcast episode, or a mock (scripted) debate among scientists.

Your final project may take several forms. Regardless of format, it must clearly explain the controversy, summarize competing evidence, propose a follow-up study, and explain how that study would move the debate forward.

AI use

In class, we will decide together what kinds of AI use, if any, are permissible for your final project. If we decide that some AI use is permissible, you must include an AI use statement with your submission.
